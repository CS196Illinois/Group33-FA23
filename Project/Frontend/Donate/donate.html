Ethics and Bill of Rights

Our Non-profit Organization conducts all fundraising and advancement activities in accordance with the Association of Fundraising Professional (AFP) Code of Ethical Principles and Standards. All interactions with donors and/or prospective donors are managed with strict adherence to the principles expressed in the AFP Donor Bill of Rights.

A Donor of Bill of Rights

At the heart of our mission lies the genuine belief in the transformative power of voluntary goodwill for societal progress. The act of giving, rooted deeply in our ethos, has always been central to enriching the fabric of our communities. To ensure that our philanthropic endeavors consistently garner the trust and esteem of the public, and to provide donors and potential supporters unwavering confidence in our cause and operations, we firmly uphold the following donor rights:

1. Transparency in Purpose: Donors have the right to be apprised of our organization's goals, the allocation and utilization of donated funds, and our commitment to maximizing the impact of each contribution.
2. Accountable Leadership: Donors deserve to know who comprises our governing body, expecting them to judiciously manage and oversee organizational resources.
3. Financial Integrity: We provide all donors with access to our latest financial documentation, underscoring our commitment to transparency.
4. Purposeful Allocation: Every donor should be confident that their contributions are channeled towards the causes they passionately support.
5. Acknowledgement: Each gift is met with appropriate gratitude, recognizing the altruism behind every contribution.
6. Confidentiality: We respect the privacy of our donors. All donation-related information is handled with the utmost discretion, adhering to all legal standards of confidentiality.
7. Professional Relationships: Donors can expect that any interaction with our representatives, be it for information or solicitation, will always be of the highest professional standard.
8. Clarity in Presentation: Those soliciting donations on our behalf will always make it clear whether they are volunteers, organization staff, or third-party fundraisers.
9. Respecting Preferences: Donors have the absolute right to request the removal of their details from any mailing lists we might share.
10. Open Communication: We encourage donors to seek clarity on any aspect of their donation, assuring them of prompt and candid responses.
